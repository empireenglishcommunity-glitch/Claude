## 1. Executive Summary

**Mission:** To build the one English learning system engineered specifically for Arabic speakers — taking them from zero to a clear, confident, fully intelligible American accent — through a custom app, an Arabic-aware AI engine, and a 24/7 community, independent of any single instructor.

### 1.1 The Five-Layer Architecture

- **Layer 0 — Unified Learner Profile (the backbone):** one record per learner — level, sub-level, all scores, streak, accent profile, error history, recordings. Every layer reads/writes here. All personalization flows from this.
- **Layer 1 — Progression Engine:** 4 levels, each with 3 sub-levels, plus a parallel Arabic-L1 accent track.
- **Layer 2 — Execution Engine:** the daily loop, with Core (~25 min) and Full paths.
- **Layer 3 — Community Engine:** in-app feed, voice rooms, accountability, mentorship.
- **Layer 4 — AI Engine:** Speech Engine (pronunciation/accent scoring) + Language Engine (LLM: writing, feedback, content, conversation).
- **Acquisition layer:** Telegram funnel bot → discovery, pricing, payment, routes new members into the app.

### 1.2 Key Differentiators
1. Built for Arabic speakers specifically (accent engine targets their exact error patterns).
2. A custom app, not a borrowed platform — full ownership of experience and brand.
3. An AI engine that actually hears you (real pronunciation scoring, not faked from transcript).
4. Honest, deliverable promises (clarity & confidence guaranteed; native-like as the rare ceiling).
5. Designed for consistency (Core path keeps streaks alive on busy days — the retention engine).
6. Arabic-first interface for beginners; English content throughout.

## 2. The Progression Engine

### 2.1 Level Overview

| Level | Name | Duration | Sub-levels | Daily (Core / Full) |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| 0 | Absolute Beginner | 8–12 wks | 0.1 · 0.2 · 0.3 | 20 min / 1.5–2 hr |
| 1 | Survival English | 10–14 wks | 1.1 · 1.2 · 1.3 | 25 min / 2–2.5 hr |
| 2 | Communication | 12–16 wks | 2.1 · 2.2 · 2.3 | 25 min / 2.5–3 hr |
| 3 | Fluency & Accent | Ongoing | Silver · Gold · Platinum | 30 min / 3 hr+ |

Each level has 3 sub-levels with micro-badges (felt progress monthly). Provisional advancement applies between full levels (pass all but one dimension → move up provisionally with the weak skill flagged). Level 3 / Mastery gates remain strict and unprovisioned.

### 2.2 The Parallel Arabic-L1 Accent Track (centerpiece)

| Level | Accent Focus | Target sounds |
|-------|--------------|---------------|
| 0 | "Impossible" consonants/vowels | /p/–/b/ (park/bark), /v/–/f/ (very/ferry), /ŋ/ (sing), /θ/–/ð/ (think/this) |
| 1 | Vowel distinctions & word stress | /ɪ/–/iː/ (ship/sheep), /æ/–/ʌ/ (cat/cut), /ʊ/–/uː/ (full/fool), 2–3 syllable stress |
| 2 | Clusters, linking & schwa | "street/spring" onsets, vowel reduction, connected speech |
| 3 | Native polish | flap-T (water/butter), dark-L (feel/call), intonation, stress-timing |

Placement diagnoses which target sounds the learner struggles with on day one and seeds their personal accent plan.

## 3. Placement & Entry System (Arabic-Aware)

### 3.1 The Diagnostic (~40 min, 5 modules)
1. Speaking (10 min) — read-aloud + 3 spontaneous prompts → fluency, grammar, vocab.
2. **Accent Diagnostic (8 min)** — targeted Arabic-L1 sound set, measured by the Speech Engine.
3. Listening (8 min) — comprehension at 80 / 120 / 160 WPM.
4. Vocabulary (7 min) — frequency-band size estimate.
5. Grammar (7 min) — tense/structure awareness.

### 3.2 Scoring & Assignment
- Each module → 0–100 → mapped to a level (0–3). Majority rules; **Speaking is the tiebreaker.**
- 20+ point gap between dimensions → flag for review.
- The accent diagnostic does NOT affect level placement — it seeds the personal accent plan.

### 3.3 Onboarding — First 48 Hours
Telegram funnel bot (discovery → pricing → payment → account) → app welcome + placement → level + accent profile + plan → buddy + first Core-path day → orientation voice session → 48-hour check-in. **Success metric:** 90% complete placement + first Core day within 48 hours.

## 4. The Execution Engine — The Daily Loop

### 4.1 Two Paths
- **🔥 Core Path (~20–30 min):** Accent Drill (Arabic-L1 target sound) + Speaking Mission (1 recording → AI feedback). A Core day = full streak credit.
- **⚔️ Full Path (~1.5–3 hr):** Accent Drill, Vocabulary, Shadowing, Speaking Mission, Listening Immersion, Writing Practice, Community.

### 4.2 Why this is the retention engine
The streak only requires the Core path — a busy learner still does ~25 minutes and keeps their fire. The Full path is always available and rewarded (bonus points, faster sub-level progress). The two non-negotiables are accent + speaking — the two things that move the needle and define the brand.

### 4.3 Daily Time by Level (Core / Full)

| Task | L0 | L1 | L2 | L3 |
|------|----|----|----|----|
| Core path total | ~20 min | ~25 min | ~25 min | ~30 min |
| Full path total | 1.5–2 hr | 2–2.5 hr | 2.5–3 hr | 3 hr+ |
| Accent share (Full) | 30 min | 40 min | 50 min | 60 min |

### 4.4 Execution Rules
Accent first; record everything (→ Speech Engine → feedback → profile); speaking before listening (Full); community is the application layer; weekends use an Intensive Protocol (review, extended shadowing, longer speaking piece, community event).

### 4.5 Connectivity-Resilient Design (Refinement #3)
The recording-heavy loop is engineered for MENA mobile reality: compressed lightweight audio, a low-data mode, downloadable/offline lessons, and graceful handling of unstable connections. AI evaluations are queued and sent efficiently.

## 5. The Arabic-L1 American Accent Engine (Centerpiece)

### 5.1 Foundational Insight
Arabic speakers' English errors are systematic and predictable — therefore systematically fixable.

| Category | Problem (Arabic L1) | Examples |
|----------|--------------------|----------|
| Missing consonants | No /p/ → /b/; no /v/ → /f/ | park→bark, very→ferry |
| Confused consonants | /ŋ/, /tʃ/ vs /ʃ/, /ʒ/ | sing, chair/share, vision |
| Forbidden clusters | vowel inserted in onsets | street→"istreet", spring→"isbring" |
| Missing vowel pairs | /ɪ/–/iː/, /æ/–/ʌ/–/ɑ/, /ʊ/–/uː/ | ship/sheep, cat/cut/cart |
| Schwa & reduction | every vowel given full value | about→/əˈbaʊt/ (the #1 giveaway) |
| Stress & rhythm | syllable-timing vs stress-timing | flattens sentence music |

### 5.2 4-Phase Progression + Dialect-Aware Coaching (Refinement #6)
- **L0:** /p/–/b/, /v/–/f/, /ŋ/, /θ/–/ð/. **Dialect-aware:** MSA speakers retain /θ/ /ð/; many Egyptian/Levantine speakers merge them to /s/–/z/ or /t/–/d/ — coaching adapts to the learner's dialect tendency.
- **L1:** vowel distinctions + word stress.
- **L2:** clusters, linking, schwa (the "heavy accent" dissolves here).
- **L3:** flap-T, dark-L, intonation, stress-timing rhythm.

### 5.3 How the Speech Engine Scores It
For every recording: Speech Engine returns per-phoneme accuracy, word-stress placement, fluency, and an overall pronunciation score (0–100). The Language Engine then translates raw scores into warm, Arabic-aware coaching with bilingual mechanics tips for L0–L1. Results write to the Layer 0 profile; tomorrow's drill auto-targets the weakest sound.

### 5.4 Shadowing System (5 phases)
Phase 1 Blind (L0) → Phase 2 Text-aided (L0–1) → Phase 3 Full-speed (L1–2) → Phase 4 Transcription (L2) → Phase 5 Advanced/emotion (L3). Every clip stresses the level's target sounds; the Speech Engine compares the learner's shadow to the native track.

### 5.5 The Accent Score & Before/After Replay
Always-visible Accent Score (0–100) broken into the learner's personal target-sound hit list, plus word stress, linking, rhythm, intonation. The **Before/After Replay** (Day-1 placement vs today) is a permanent feature — the most motivating moment in the system and the most powerful marketing asset.

### 5.6 Coaching Tone (bilingual scaffolding that fades)
L0–L1: English-first with short Arabic clarifications for sound mechanics. L2: Arabic fades to rare. L3: English-only (full immersion).

## 6. Learning Materials Library (AI-Generated)

| Component | What it is | V2.0 upgrade |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Grammar (pattern-first) | Pattern cards, not rules | Examples use learner's own vocabulary/interests |
| Vocabulary | Frequency-based, leveled | Spaced repetition (Day 2/4/7/14/30), Arabic logical meaning |
| Speaking templates | Ready frameworks | Auto-filled with learner's real context |
| Writing frameworks | Structural scaffolds | Feedback loop targets the learner's errors |
| Cheat sheets | One-page references | Personal Error Log generated from their mistakes |
| Pronunciation Reference ⭐ | In-app dictionary tool | US audio + syllable/stress + IPA + Arabic logical meaning + example |

**Grammar philosophy:** Exposure → Imitation → Correction → Reference (rules only when asked).
**Vocabulary targets:** L0: 500 · L1: +1,000 · L2: +1,500 · L3: unlimited. 5-step acquisition: Encounter → Definition (simple English) → Image/Context → Production (3 spoken sentences, Speech-checked) → Spaced Review. Arabic *logical* meaning, never literal.
**Cultural-Appropriateness Filter (Refinement #2):** all immersion/example content is family-safe and culturally respectful (no profanity/sexual/conflicting themes). V1.0's media list is replaced with vetted, wholesome alternatives.
**Arabic-First Interface (Refinement #1):** UI, onboarding, navigation, and instructions in Arabic for L0–L1 (content stays English); English-toggle becomes default as learners climb.

## 7. The Community Engine (In-App, Not Discord)

### 7.1 Community Spaces
Level Rooms (L0–L3), Voice Rooms (live + open), The Feed (showcases, before/after), Accountability Hub (streaks, check-in, leaderboards), Help & Q&A (L0–1 may use Arabic here), Mentorship.

### 7.2 English-Immersion Rule
Voice & main rooms = English only. One exception: the Help & Q&A space lets L0–1 ask "how do I say ___?" in Arabic so beginners aren't silenced into quitting.

### 7.3 Live Voice Rooms (scheduled + open)
Morning Warm-Up (All), Accent Drill (L0–1), Scenario Practice (L1), Debate Club (L2–3), Shadowing Circle (L1–2), Story Night (L2–3), Evening Wind-Down (All). Voice rooms are technically hardest — phased: text + async voice notes first, live rooms layered in once the core loop is proven.

### 7.4 Accountability & Gamification
Streaks reward the Core path; points, badges, weekly leaderboards (Most Consistent, Biggest Improvement, Most Helpful, Community MVP). Mentorship ladder: New Member → Onboarding Buddy → Peer Mentor → Ambassador → Moderator. Intervention protocol: nudges at 1 missed day → buddy at 2 → mentor at 3 → re-engagement at 5+.

### 7.5 Rhythm-of-Life Scheduling (Refinement #5)
Voice-room schedules and streak rules respect prayer times and adapt during Ramadan (post-Iftar/Suhoor sessions, flexible "Ramadan streak" so fasting learners aren't penalized).

## 8. Evaluation & Progress Tracking

### 8.1 Weekly Assessment (~30 min)

| Component | Weight | Engine |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Speaking Fluency | 30% | Speech + Language |
| Listening Comprehension | 20% | Language |
| Accent Assessment | 15% | Speech |
| Vocabulary Recall | 15% | Language |
| Writing Sample | 10% | Language |
| Task Completion | 10% | Profile (Core or Full counts) |

→ Weekly Performance Score (WPS, 0–100). Ranges: 90–100 Excellent → invite to mentor; 80–89 Strong; 70–79 Satisfactory; 60–69 At Risk (auto check-in); <60 Critical (coaching trigger).

### 8.2 Monthly Review
Auto-generated from the profile: score trend, dimension breakdown vs last month, strengths/weaknesses, consistency, community participation, Level Readiness %, AI recommendations. Short voice review with a mentor.

### 8.3 Advancement & Sub-Level Engine
Sub-level micro-exams (~every 3–4 weeks) → climb 1.1→1.2→1.3 + badge. Full Level Advancement Exam: Live Speaking 80% · Listening 75% · Vocabulary 80% · Accent 75% · Writing 80%. Provisional advancement for a single missed dimension. Level 3 / Mastery (Silver/Gold/Platinum) strict and unprovisioned.

### 8.4 Progress Dashboard (reads from Layer 0)
Overall progress bar, 12-week score chart, 5-axis radar, streak counter, Accent Score with personal target-sound hit list, before/after replay, time invested, community stats, next-milestone countdown, anonymous percentile.

## 11. The AI Engine — Technical Specification

### 11.1 Two Engines
- **🎙️ Speech Engine** — input: learner audio; output: per-phoneme accuracy, word-stress, fluency, overall pronunciation score (0–100). Tech: specialized pronunciation-assessment API (e.g., Azure Pronunciation Assessment / Speechace / equivalent) — swappable.
- **🧠 Language Engine** — input: transcripts, writing, scores, profile; output: writing correction, Arabic-aware coaching, content generation, conversation, feedback synthesis. Tech: GPT-4 / Claude-class LLM — swappable.

### 11.2 The Feedback Pipeline
Record → Speech Engine (the "what") → Language Engine (the "how to fix," Arabic-aware, bilingual at L0–L1) → Unified Learner Profile updated → tomorrow's drill auto-targets the weakest sound. An LLM alone cannot hear /v/ vs /f/ (reads text only); the Speech Engine catches it, the LLM explains it.

### 11.3 Abstraction Layer (no vendor lock-in)
All AI calls route through our own internal interface, not directly to a vendor. Providers can be swapped behind the interface with zero app disruption.

### 11.4 Cost Model
Speech evaluations: pay-per-use, cents per recording, controlled by tier limits. LLM: pay-per-token, batch content generation, cache reusable lessons, premium models reserved for feedback. Per-member AI cost stays inside the feasibility model and scales linearly, not exponentially.

### 11.5 Human-in-the-Loop → Automated
Pilot: spot-check AI output. As accuracy is validated, progressively remove the human: AI assisted → automated → autonomous.

### 11.6 Prompt Library
The 25 V1.0 prompts (cheat sheets, speaking missions, evaluation, content generation, scaling) become the Language Engine's instruction set, with variables auto-filled from the Unified Learner Profile.
